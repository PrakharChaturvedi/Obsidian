## How would you manage unstable requirements in agile? [7 marks]
1. **Customer Input Throughout the Development Process:**
    - Customers and stakeholders provide feedback continuously, not just at the beginning and end of the project.
    - Early feedback on features helps manage scope and prioritize requirements.
    - Agile teams can take risks and innovate based on feedback without significant time or budget loss.
    - Teams aim to deliver a working product at the end of each sprint.
2. **Product Backlog Sets Development Priorities:**
    - The product backlog is a prioritized list of features for the team to build.
    - It is managed by different roles (e.g., scrum master, product manager) and allows input from the team, stakeholders, and customers.
    - Regular grooming of the backlog helps prioritize features and manage changing requirements.
3. **Daily Meetings Promote Communication:**
    - Daily stand-up meetings help the team share progress and identify obstacles.
    - Issues and feedback about requirements can be discussed immediately.
    - Open communication allows team members and management to quickly address changing requirements.
4. **Task Boards Make Developer Tasks and Details Visible:**
    - Task boards visually divide tasks into stages: To Do, In Progress, In Testing, Done.
    - They provide visibility of project status and dependencies, making it easier to manage changing requirements.
    - Cloud-based project management platforms offer easy access to task boards.
5. **User Stories and Sprints Orchestrate Change:**
    - User stories describe features or requirements and guide development during sprints.
    - Feedback can lead to new stories or changes in existing ones during the sprint process.
    - This iterative approach allows for continuous adjustments and improvements based on changing requirements.


## How would you demonstrate the story card maturity model (SMMM) [3 marks]
- The Story Card Maturity Model (SMMM) is a framework for assessing and improving the quality and maturity of user stories (or story cards) in an Agile development process.
- Steps : 
	1. **Define Maturity Levels:** Establish different levels of story card maturity, ranging from low to high quality. For example, you can categorize levels such as Initial, Defined, Managed, and Optimized.
	2. **Identify Criteria for Each Level:** Determine the criteria for each maturity level, such as clarity of user stories, completeness, acceptance criteria, stakeholder involvement, and alignment with project goals.
	3. **Assess User Stories:** Evaluate user stories against the defined criteria to determine their current maturity level. This assessment helps identify areas where improvement is needed.
	4. **Provide Feedback and Training:** Share assessment results with the team and provide feedback on how to improve user stories. Offer training sessions or workshops on writing better user stories, including clarity, completeness, and prioritization.
	5. **Implement Improvement Plans:** Work with the team to create and execute plans to improve the maturity of user stories. This may involve refining existing stories, writing new ones, and incorporating feedback from stakeholders.
	6. **Monitor Progress and Iterate:** Continuously monitor the progress of story card maturity and assess the impact of improvements on the development process. Use this information to iterate and refine the assessment and improvement process as needed.
- By demonstrating the Story Card Maturity Model, teams can improve the quality of their user stories, leading to better communication, clearer requirements, and more efficient development processes.

## Describe KM in software engineering. [3 marks]
-   Knowledge Management (KM) in software engineering involves the systematic process of capturing, sharing, and utilizing knowledge within software development teams and organizations. The goal of KM is to ensure that the expertise, experience, and information gained throughout the software engineering lifecycle are accessible and used effectively to improve project outcomes and team performance. 
- Here are some key aspects of KM in software engineering:
	 1. **Capturing Knowledge:** This involves documenting best practices, lessons learned, code snippets, design patterns, and other useful information during the development process. Tools like wikis, repositories, and documentation systems can help capture this knowledge.
	2. **Sharing Knowledge:** Knowledge sharing encourages collaboration and information exchange among team members. Techniques such as code reviews, pair programming, and regular team meetings facilitate the sharing of knowledge and insights.
	3. **Reusing Knowledge:** By making knowledge readily available, teams can avoid reinventing the wheel and reuse existing solutions, code, or designs. This improves efficiency and reduces the time and effort needed to solve problems.
	4. **Leveraging Expertise:** Identifying and utilizing the expertise of team members can enhance project quality and speed. Experienced team members can mentor others, provide guidance, and offer solutions to complex challenges.
	5. **Continuous Learning:** KM promotes a culture of continuous learning and improvement. Teams can use knowledge from previous projects to refine processes, avoid past mistakes, and implement more effective strategies.


## What do you mean by concurrency in agile requirements generation? [1 marks]
- Concurrency in Agile requirements generation refers to the practice of gathering and refining requirements in parallel with development activities, allowing requirements to evolve continuously as the project progresses.

## How would you define agile requirements prioritization? [1 marks]
- Agile requirements prioritization is the process of ranking user stories or features based on their value to stakeholders, business goals, and potential impact on the project, allowing teams to focus on delivering the most important work first.
